- On KIPRIS patent-transfer prediction, no learned model beats a popularity baseline.
- Failure is traced to popularity bias and a 91.7% patent cold-start regime.
- A strict tie-break inflates degenerate cold-start models to near-perfect scores.
- A random split reverses the verdict, making matrix factorization the best model.
- Standard popularity-bias mitigations give only partial, unstable gains.
